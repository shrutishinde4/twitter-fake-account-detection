Distribution of Account and Tweet Types

Initial data has a total of 10,197 unique users which are classified into three main categories:

  • Real users: Genuine Twitter users
  • Fake users: Users that hide their real identities which follow real users to increase their popularity
  • Spam users: Users that post information/tweets with an agenda of increasing interest in a particular topic

Source Distribution of Real, Fake, Spam Tweets

A good indicator to classify accounts as real, fake, or spam:

  • Real users mostly use Twitter mobile apps to post tweets
  • Fake accounts and spam bots mostly use web or 3rd party spamming software. We can assume some kinds of automation have been used to post tweets

Time Distribution of Real, Fake, Spam Tweets

Another good indicator to classify accounts as real, fake, or spam:

  • For real tweets, there is a clear indication that during 9h00-15h00, there is a passivenesss of activities
  • Activities for fake accounts and spam bots seem to happen more after 15h00

WordCloud for Real, Fake, and Spam Tweets

Here are the wordcloud graphs for each tweet types:

  • Real tweets keywords are mainly in English
  • Seems like fake and spam tweets tend to be more in different foreign languages such as Spanish, Italian, etc.

Betweenness Centrality as Node Size

Betweenness centrality captures the importance of a node for the flow of information within the network. Are there some correlation between account type and betweennness centrality?

  • A node betweenness measure is represented by the node size, so the bigger the node the higher its betweenness
  • Most of the bigger nodes are blue, and many nodes seem to disappear from the map because their betweeness centrality is so small
  • It seems that a high betweenness measure is a good indicator for a real account

Betweenness Centrality as Color Scheme and Out-Degree/In-Degree

Is there a relationship between betweenness centrality and out-degree centrality or in-degree centrality?

  • Out-degree: describes how many edges originate from that node or how many times that account is retweeted by other users
  • In-degree: describes how many times a node is a sink node or how many times a user retweets other accounts
